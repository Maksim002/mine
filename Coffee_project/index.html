Coffee

Coffee is a brewed drink prepared from roasted coffee beans, the seeds of berries from certain Coffea species.
Once ripe, coffee berries are picked, processed, and dried.
Roasted beans are ground and then brewed with near-boiling water to produce the beverage known as coffee.

Cappuccino

Outside of Italy, cappuccino is a coffee drink that today is typically composed of a single espresso shot and hot milk, with the surface topped with foamed milk.

Espresso

Espresso is a full-flavored, concentrated form of coffee that is served in “shots.” It is made by forcing pressurized hot water through very finely ground coffee beans using an espresso machine.

Latte

Latte is made with six to eight ounces of steamed milk and one shot of espresso. Larger lattes are often made with a double shot of espresso. The beverage may also be topped with foam for both flavor and presentation. A latte is similar to a cappuccino, but it has less foamed milk and more steamed milk.
